The gap

TTS speaks “Indian English.” India speaks in districts.

Commercial voice AI offers one or two Indian voices — a generic Hindi, an accentless English. But Bhojpuri from Ara doesn’t sound like Magahi from Gaya, and Kannada in Bidar doesn’t sound like Kannada in Mysore. Vaani recorded that reality: spontaneous, image-prompted speech from real people in 165 districts, released CC-BY-4.0.

Meanwhile Voicebox put zero-shot voice cloning — Chatterbox, Qwen3-TTS, seven engines — on the desktop, MIT-licensed, no cloud required. All it needs is a good reference clip. Vaani has 31,000 hours of them.

How it works

From field recording to studio voice in four steps.

STEP 1

Stream

Pick any of 165 State–District configs. Audio streams from Hugging Face — nothing near the 3.6 TB corpus touches your disk.

STEP 2

Curate

Every clip is scored for cloning quality — duration, loudness, SNR, speech ratio, clipping — and grouped by speaker. Field noise gets rejected; keepers get ranked.

STEP 3

Export

Selected speakers become voice packs: reference WAVs, metadata, and CC-BY attribution, ready for Voicebox’s Add Voice flow.

STEP 4

Clone

Voicebox (or the built-in Chatterbox preview) speaks any text in that voice — Hindi, Tamil, Bengali, or accented English. Locally.

Where this goes

Four directions worth building next.

Accent Atlas

A clickable map of India where every district speaks. Tourism, education, and film localization with real regional texture.

An Indian engine for Voicebox

Fine-tune Chatterbox or Qwen3-TTS on Vaani’s 2,043 transcribed hours and contribute the engine upstream.

Dialect-aware dictation

Voicebox dictates through Whisper, which stumbles on Indian accents and code-switching. Vaani is exactly the fine-tuning data it needs.

A first voice for 100+ languages

Mundari, Kurukh, Bhili and dozens more have no TTS anywhere. A curated pack gives each one a synthetic voice for the first time.

Try the prototype

A working studio, today.

The VaaniBox prototype ships a Gradio app with explore/curate, one-click voice-pack export, and an optional cloning preview. No Hugging Face account? Demo mode runs the full pipeline on synthetic voices.

# in the vaanibox project folder
uv venv --python 3.12 .venv
uv pip install --python .venv/bin/python -r requirements.txt
.venv/bin/python app.py
# → http://127.0.0.1:5051  (landing + studio)

Open by construction. Vaani is CC-BY-4.0 (ARTPARK, IISc Bangalore); every exported pack carries attribution. Voicebox and VaaniBox are MIT.

These are real people’s voices. Use packs for accent-authentic narration, research, accessibility, and language preservation — never impersonation. VaaniBox exists to give India’s spoken diversity a place in synthetic speech, not to counterfeit any individual.
